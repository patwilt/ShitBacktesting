# Average Australian Couple: Financial Impact of Having 2 Kids

**Assumptions**
- Couple aged: 28–35
- Combined household income: ~$180k/year
- Two children (2–3 year gap)
- Public schooling
- Middle-class lifestyle
- No major health complications

---

## Before Kids

| Category | Typical |
|-----------|----------|
| Household income | ~$180k |
| Savings/investing capacity | ~$20k–50k/year |
| Lifestyle flexibility | High |
| Housing | Smaller apartment/home |

Typical phase:
- Wealth accumulation accelerates
- Strong investment growth potential
- Travel and discretionary spending higher

---

## Pregnancy → First Year

### Major cost driver:
**Reduced household income**

| Item | Approx impact |
|--------|---------------:|
| Reduced work / parental leave | $20k–80k+ |
| Baby setup (cot, pram, car seat etc.) | $3k–10k |
| Medical costs | $0–10k+ |
| Ongoing baby expenses | $2k–5k/year |

Notes:
- Baby gear is usually a smaller cost than expected
- Temporary loss of income often dominates

---

## Age 1–5: Highest Financial Pressure

### Major cost driver:
**Childcare**

| Item | Approx annual |
|--------|--------------:|
| Childcare (after subsidy) | $8k–25k+ |
| Food/clothes/toys | $3k–6k |
| Health | $500–2k |
| Activities | $500–2k |

For two children:

| Category | Approx annual |
|------------|--------------:|
| Childcare total | $15k–40k+ |

Typical effects:
- Savings often slow dramatically or temporarily stop
- Career flexibility reduces
- FIRE timelines commonly shift several years

---

## Age 5–12: Recovery Period

| Item | Approx annual |
|--------|--------------:|
| School expenses | $1k–5k |
| Sports/clubs | $1k–5k |
| Food | Increasing |
| Housing costs | Increasing |

Typical effects:
- Childcare burden drops
- Household income recovers
- Savings and investing restart

---

## Age 12–18: Teen Years

| Item | Approx annual |
|--------|--------------:|
| Food | High |
| Technology | $500–2k |
| Activities/social costs | $2k–10k+ |
| Transport | Increasing |

Typical effects:
- Costs shift rather than disappear
- Education and activities become larger expenses

---

# Approximate Lifetime Cost (2 Kids)

Estimated direct + indirect cost to age 18:

**~$700k–$1M+**

Large variables:
- Private vs public school
- Housing decisions
- Childcare duration
- Career interruptions
- Lifestyle choices

---

# Financial Timeline

### Age 25–30
- Wealth building accelerates

### Age 30–40
- Savings slow or stall
- Childcare + reduced income dominate

### Age 40–50
- Income typically recovers
- Children become more independent
- Wealth accumulation often accelerates again

---

# Key Takeaway

Most people expect:
> "Kids are expensive because of stuff."

Most of the financial impact usually comes from:

1. Reduced earnings
2. Childcare costs
3. Larger housing requirements
4. Opportunity cost (lost super + investment growth)

The biggest effect is often a temporary reduction in household savings rate rather than permanent financial damage.
